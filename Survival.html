Upon introspection, survival was unbelievable.

Was it God?

Was it determination or sheer luck?

One thing was quite certain, life had been one hell of a ride. Once homeless with lots of friends, it was hard to comprehend.

To survive, you have to be a fox to recognize the traps and a lion to frighten the wolves; what if you are in the wild unprepared?

In the streets where there is no love, evident of the street mantra, “no padi for jungle”, toughness is learned - sometimes you want love and affection, who best to give that if not the mother?

Indulging in weed and drugs, the realization of its slow destruction set in. Nevertheless, it was a welcome cope.

A seeming fox, frightening like a lion is dangerous, one lapse and its death, but with bravado and luck, survival might be just around the corner.

Sometimes the allure of love was beautiful but it quite often turned sour when comfort set in, which taught a single lesson, if it’s not the mother, it’s often not genuine.

Slowly and steadily andvancing, the hope of getting love and comfort from the assured genuine source whenever its needed provided succour, unfortunately death had a different plan. At a time the father struggled with pain and regret, painful lessons of pride and humility were learnt.

Losing that one genuine love was quite tough, very quickly it was realized comfort is within.
